The History of Chinese Chess



XiangQi is a popular board game in China, it is in the same family class as Western or International Chess. When you visit China, you can often see it is being played in parks or simply a spot that has enough space for the chessboard to place on top, with people surrounded the two players watching their intense match. Currently, there are many theories on the origin of XiangQi - 象棋 (Chinese Chess). According to the book "A History of Chess" by H. J. R. Murray published in 1913, chess was first invented in India, it then travelled to Persia. After that, it went to China, and to Korea and Japan. The earliest chess pieces were found at Afrasiab, Uzbekistan believed to be dated around 706 AD since a coin was also found near the chess pieces dated 706 AD. For XiangQi, it was first referenced in "The Book of Marvels", a book by Nui Seng-ju who passed away in 847 AD Lets now look at some possible origins of XiangQi.

XiangQi Was Developed Based on the Game By Hán Xin - 韩信



One possible origin of XiangQi is from Hán Xin, a military general who served Liu Bang during the Chu-Han Contention and he is one of the best military general in the history of China. During a battle, Hán Xin was required to hold his position with his troops in winter, he decided to create a game or chess to represent the battle so he could practise his battle strategies. A few years after that battle, he fell out of favour with Liu Bang (the emperor of the Hang Dynasty) and the game became less popular. But in the Tang Dynasty, a few hundred years later, the game was revived with some changes in its rule.






XiangQi was Originated from Ancient China



Another possible case is that XiangQi was originated from the chess game developed during the Spring and Autumn Period, which is a period with a lots of war happening. This theory is based on the rules of XiangQi since they are closely related to some of the strategies used in battle. For example, blocking the path of the horse by placing a chess piece infront of the horse (tripping its leg). During the Tang Dynasty, Xiang （相) chess pieces were added into the game. Xiang chess pieces are located next to another animal piece horse in the starting position, and since elephant (象）and the foreign minister （相) both has the same pronunciation, the Xiang green chess pieces were replaced by elephant pieces. When the chess was taken from China to Persia and India, the xiang chess pieces lost their original meaning of foreign minister and became an elephant. Hence, throughout Persia, northern India there was a chess game with a piece called "elephant" or "alfil" in Arabic.




The left-hand image means foreign minister and it is located next to an animal chess piece (horse) at the starting position, and since elephant (the right-hand image) has the same pronunciation in Chinese, the green 相 (foreign minister) chess piece was gradually replaced by the word 象 (elephant).






XiangQi was Originated from India



As mentioned before, some people said chess was originated from the North Western part of India. Chaturanga is thought to be the ancestor of Chinese Chess, which was invented independently in the 5th or 6th century. There are many variation of Chaturanga including two player Chaturanga and four player Chaturanga. The chess was then taken to Persia and Shatranj was developed, which formed the bases for the international or western chess we played today. At the end, the chess travelled to China from Persia and formed the bases for XiangQi.
